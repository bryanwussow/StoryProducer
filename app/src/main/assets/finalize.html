Finalize

Who: The primary translator or phone manager along with anybody else interested in video production.

Purpose: In the Finalize phase, the user must complete the local credits slide, make final decisions about the new videos that will be created, and create videos for the various audiences with whom you will share the videos. 

Activities: 

  1. Complete the Local Credits slide. Tap the EDIT LOCAL CREDITS button to open an editable pop-up window.  Type in the names of people who were involved in the various activities of translation, song composition, photography, voice acting, etc. Add a phone or email for people to contact if they want to get more videos or if they have questions about the story. Tap SAVE to close the window and return to Finalize.  The grey tick/check mark in the circle will turn green when the local credits slide has been edited.

  2. Type a short story name (in your local language) on the appropriate line.  This will appear as part of the file name of the video to assist you in identifying the content of the video.  (The number of the story will be automatically added to the video file name.)  This text can be changed whenever you want.  The grey tick/check mark in the circle will turn green when you have entered text on this line.

  3. Choose options to be included in the video.  Tap the square box for each option to add a tick mark and make it active.  Tap it again to remove the tick mark.

  4. Create your new video.  Tap the CREATE VIDEO button and wait for the phone app to generate the new videos.  Do not do other activities on the phone while the video is being created. This will take 1 to 30 minutes depending on how long the story and song are.  You can watch the progress bar to see progress.  The app will automatically and simultaneously create your story into two types of video formats: mp4 and 3gp. 

  5. Recreate your video.  You have the ability to create several versions of your story video.  Select different options or features to produce multiple versions of your story.

Notes:

  • The file name for each video will be auto-generated by the app:  a 3-digit number taken from the number of the story template + a short story name in the language typed by the user in the Finalize phase + a 3-letter ethnologue code for the language taken from the registration language information + various short 2-letter codes indicating what options or features are in the video (Fx = background music and effects; Sg = local song; Px = pictures; Mv = movement on the pictures; Tx = story text / subtitles)

  • The 3gp format is a low resolution video created to play on the tiny screens of feature phones.  These videos are relatively small and do not take up too much storage space -- averaging about 20 MBs per story video, but it will appear “grainy” or “pixelated” on a smartphone screen. 

  • The mp4 format is a higher resolution video created to be viewed on larger screens and also projected.  These videos are larger and each require an average of 200 MBs for storage. A higher resolution (mp4) video is necessary to be able to read text captions / transcriptions on the screen.

  • Here are some examples of the types of media Story Producer can create:

    • For a movie, include the options for Pictures and Picture Movement (with or without Background music).

    • For a talking book - include the options for Pictures and Story Text, without Picture Movement (with or without background music). You of course need to have entered story text captions/transcriptions that match the audio recordings. 

    • Still slides with text captions (like a book, plus auto page turns) - include the options for Pictures and Story Text (if you entered story text captions, and play it back at zero audio volume)

    • Audio narration with background music - by turning off Pictures and Story Text, so there are no visuals

    • All of the above with or without an appended local song.
